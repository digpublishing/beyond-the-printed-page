--- title: 'Off the Press: Electronic Publishing in the Arts' date: 2014-05-28 tags: - open web-based publishing - other conferences published: true author: Greg Albers ---

In March 2013, The Institute of Network Cultures of Amsterdam University in Rotterdam put together a consortium of publishers, cultural workers and technologists in a research project called the Digital Publishing Toolkit. As they state it, they seek to answer: “In what way can a platform be created with new tools for open source-publishing, by which publishers in the art- and cultural sector can produce interactive e-publications by themselves?”

Last week, May 22–23, 2014, they held a conference, Off the Press: Electronic Publishing in the Arts, exploring their work so far and that of others in the field working toward the same basic goals.

Short blog accounts of many of the talks have been posted and it looks like there may be more to come, as well as some video coverage. I'll update this post with that when/if it comes. In the meantime, if you're interested in digital publishing in the arts, or in open and experimental publishing in general, you have some reading to do.

  • Networks as Agents in the Clash Between Personal and Industrial Post-Digital Print, Alessandro Ludovico
  • CatalanTalk: Publishing Multilingual Interviews, from Twitter to Ebooks to Paper, Elizabeth Castro
  • Books Are Evil, or Towards a Collaborative Production Model of Books Using Free Software, Adam Hyde
  • Free Your Objects (and Let the Subject Follow), John Haltiwanger
  • Making .epubs Easy with the People's E-Book, Oliver Wise
  • Electronic Publishing in the Arts: What Works and What Doesn’t, Florian Cramer
  • Hybrid Workflows for Hybrid Publishing, Miriam Rasch
  • Digitizing Context Without Walls: Creating EPubs Through the EPubster Generator, Pia Pol

  • Setting Up an Art Digital Book Store, or "Working Out How To Do Something Is Better Than Waiting for a Magic Formula", Joaquín González
